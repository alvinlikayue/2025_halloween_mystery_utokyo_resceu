🌊 Learn More: What Are Gravitational Waves?

When massive celestial objects — like black holes or neutron stars — collide, the very fabric of space and time begins to ripple. These ripples are called gravitational waves.

First detected directly in 2015, they opened a brand-new way of “listening” to the universe and studying cosmic events invisible to light.

In Japan, the underground KAGRA observatory uses laser to detect these faint cosmic waves — the whispers of the universe itself.
