On the table was a strange diagram filled with red and blue arrows, pointing in all sorts of directions.

🧩 Explanation

The red and blue arrows each represented a different kind of direction:

  • ❤️ Red arrows: 3D orientation — Up / Down / Left / Right
  • 💙 Blue arrows: Compass directions — North / East / South / West

By taking the first letter of each direction in every horizontal row, you get:

  • Row 1 → Right, East, South, T → REST
  • Row 2 → Down, O, West, North → DOWN
  • Row 3 → Right, East, South, C, East, Up → RESCEU

That’s the “direction of the universe” Kid was talking about — the word RESCEU.

📡 Learn More: What is RESCEU?

RESCEU — the Research Center for the Early Universe — is part of the Graduate School of Science at the University of Tokyo. It studies the origins and large-scale structure of the cosmos, exploring cosmic “directions” and “fluctuations” through theoretical and observational research.

RESCEU also collaborates with observatories such as KAGRA, LIGO, and Virgo, working together to uncover the universe’s structure through waves — such as gravitational waves — and light.
